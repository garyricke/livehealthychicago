The Organizing Model

LHC is not a program — it's a coordination layer. It aligns existing assets, relationships, and energy toward a shared, measurable goal. Understanding the model is the prerequisite for all audience-specific messaging.

Three Interventions

Public Health Campaign
Spark conversation, build awareness, motivate blood pressure checks. Led by CDPH + coalition. Audiences 01–03.
Mobile Care in Community
Pharmacist + nurse + CHW team deploys to CBOs. Screenings, medication support, food access, and care connection. No appointment.
Clinical Practice Transformation
Evidence-based trainings, shared data dashboard, and QI support so clinicians can measure, manage, and track hypertension better.
How They Reinforce Each Other

Campaigns drive people to screenings → mobile care connects them to clinical partners → clinical transformation ensures that connection leads to lasting care. The interventions only work at scale when all three operate together.

Section 03

Messaging Matrix

Each audience needs a different entry point into LHC's story. This matrix maps the core message, supporting proof points, tone guidance, and calls-to-action by segment. Use it as a writing brief — not a script.

01
General Public — Blood Pressure Unknown
Chicagoans who have not had a recent BP check and may not know their numbers. Goal: spark curiosity, reduce friction, motivate a first check.
Core Message
"Most people with high blood pressure feel completely fine — it's why checking yours matters. Getting your BP checked is free, fast, and now available right in your neighborhood."
Supporting Points
  • Nearly 1 in 3 Chicagoans has high blood pressure — many don't know it
  • High BP has no symptoms, but quietly damages your heart, brain, and kidneys
  • LHC makes it easy: no appointment, no parking, no waiting room
  • Knowing your numbers is the first and most important step
  • Approved headline: "Stronger Hearts. Healthier Neighborhoods."
Proof Points
31.9%of Chicago residents have hypertension — many are undiagnosed
27priority neighborhoods with the highest cardiovascular disease rates
FreeAll LHC screenings — no insurance, no appointment, no cost
Tone
Plain language Empowering, not alarming Warm and direct
Avoid: "silent killer," "ASCVD," doom-and-gloom statistics, fear-based framing. Focus on empowerment and ease, not risk.
CTA Find a free screening → livehealthychi.com Check your numbers
02
High BP — Not Connected to Clinical Care
People who know (or suspect) they have high blood pressure but aren't regularly seeing a provider. Goal: reduce barriers, build trust, motivate that first connection to ongoing care.
Core Message
"You already know something's off. Live Healthy Chicago connects you to a care team that comes to you — right in your neighborhood — so managing your blood pressure doesn't mean navigating a system alone."
Supporting Points
  • No doctor? No insurance? No problem. LHC starts where you are
  • Community health workers who know your neighborhood and speak your language
  • Free medication support and lifestyle guidance — no strings attached
  • LHC connects you to a local health center that takes your insurance (or doesn't require it)
  • Monthly food boxes and food pantry connections — because nutrition is care
Proof Points
50%of people tested at some community screening sites have elevated BP — many for the first time
CHWsCommunity health workers embedded in neighborhoods — they help navigate insurance, PCPs, and social services
Same-dayWalk in to a screening site and leave with a care plan and follow-up scheduled
Tone
Non-judgmental Action-oriented Trust-building
Lead with ease and proximity — not risk. This audience has likely already experienced barriers. Meet that reality directly.
CTA Find a weekly screening site See the weekly schedule
03
High BP — In Clinical Care
People actively being treated for hypertension by a healthcare provider. Goal: sustain engagement, support adherence, reinforce that their effort is working and that LHC is behind them.
Core Message
"You're already doing the hardest part. Live Healthy Chicago is here to help you stay on track — with the tools, support, and community to keep your blood pressure where it belongs."
Supporting Points
  • Home BP monitoring support so you can track between appointments
  • Medication adherence resources — what to do when it's hard to stay consistent
  • Nutrition and lifestyle support that links directly to BP control
  • Community health workers as an ongoing connection point — not just a one-time visit
  • Your provider is part of the LHC network — your care team is coordinated
Proof Points
AMA MAP™LHC uses the American Medical Association's validated 7-step accurate BP measurement protocol
Home BPHome monitoring significantly improves BP control — LHC supports access to devices
HEDISLHC tracks population-level BP control rates across all clinical partners
Tone
Affirming Practical Partner-like
CTA Explore resources → livehealthychi.com Review your BP category
CBO
Community-Based Organization Administrators
Leaders at churches, community centers, and social service organizations who can host the LHC mobile care team. Goal: invite partnership, make the ask clear, remove fear of complexity.
Core Message
"Your organization is already a place your community trusts. Partnering with Live Healthy Chicago means bringing life-saving blood pressure care to the people you already serve — at no cost to you or them."
LHC's Ask of CBOs
  • Host the mobile care team for 2-hour sessions tied to your existing programming (~3 months)
  • Market the screening to your community
  • Provide a space with tables, chairs, and power outlets
  • Designate a point person and CHW to coordinate
  • Debrief with LHC team after each event
What LHC Brings
  • Full care team: pharmacist, nurse, medical assistant
  • All equipment for blood pressure measurement
  • Advance communication and coordination support
  • Monitoring and follow-up for those with high BP
  • Connection to local health centers for ongoing care
Benefits for the CBO
✓ Enhance programming ✓ Free BP training for staff ✓ Clinical partnerships ✓ Access to technical experts ✓ On-site medical support ✓ No cost to your org
Tone
Respectful of their time Clear, low-friction ask Mutual benefit
Lead with their community's need, not LHC's program goals. CBOs will engage when they see this as an enhancement to what they already do — not an external mandate.

F1

Overview of Live Healthy Chicago

For CBO administrators, community leaders, and any new partner

The Why

  • Preventing early illness and death takes more than one great program — it takes people working together
  • LHC builds on existing efforts and relationships to improve care for Chicagoans
  • Health systems, community agencies, health plans, and government leaders are uniting on common goals

The What & How

  • A coalition of community leaders, healthcare experts, government, and frontline caregivers
  • Tests new approaches, shares data, implements evidence-based trainings
  • Makes care easier to access in partnership with CBOs
  • Pilots new ways to pay for prevention

First Goal & Approach

  • Reduce the risk of heart attack and stroke across Chicago by 1% in 3 years
  • 27 neighborhoods on Chicago's West and South Sides
  • Three interventions: Public Health Campaign, Mobile Care, Clinical Practice Transformation

F2

Mobile Care Intervention

For CBO administrators — hosting the mobile team at your site

The Why

  • People are more likely to get preventive care in places they already trust — churches, barbershops, community centers
  • Your organization can help make it easier to get a BP check — the first step toward better health

LHC Mobile Team Services

  • Blood pressure screenings
  • Connection to clinical care and social services
  • Medication recommendations (under physician oversight)
  • Nutrition support + food access connections

Your CBO's Role

  • Host the team for ~2-hour sessions tied to existing programming (~3 months)
  • Market the screening to your community
  • Provide space: tables, chairs, power outlets
  • Designate a point person and CHW
  • Debrief with LHC team after each session

LHC's Role

  • Project Coordinator, Pharmacist, Nurse, Medical Assistant
  • All necessary equipment
  • Advance coordination and communication
  • Follow-up monitoring for those with high BP

Benefits for Your CBO

✓ Holistic care for your community ✓ Free BP training for staff ✓ Clinical relationships ✓ Access to expert resources ✓ On-site medical support
