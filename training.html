Updating Live Healthy Chicago

A short, practical guide for the team: how to load resources into the portal, see who has signed up, and get events and news posted. Nothing here needs code — it's all point-and-click.

What you can do

  1. Add or update resources in the portal
  2. See who has signed up (and filter by role)
  3. Get an event posted
  4. Add a news story or update
  5. Quick links & passwords

1  Add or update resources

The portal is the shared library of tools and materials for CHWs, clinicians, and partners. This is the main “update things we can share with others” task.

  1. Go to livehealthychi.com/portal and click Sign in. Enter the admin password (shared with you separately — it's different from the read-only viewer password partners use).
  2. Once you're signed in as admin, the Add resource button and the edit/delete controls appear. Click Add resource.
  3. Fill in the title, a short description, the category/section it belongs in, and the resource type (handout, video, link, etc.). Add the file or link.
  4. Set the audience tags — who the resource is for. A resource can carry several, so something useful to more than one group shows up for all of them:
    CliniciansCHWsHealth EducatorsPatients & CommunityComms / SocialAdmin / Operations
  5. Save. It appears in the library immediately. To change or remove something later, hover the card and use the edit (pencil) or delete controls.
Partners who sign in with the viewer password see the library read-only. The Add / Edit / Delete controls only show for admins, so there's no risk of a partner changing anything.

2  See who has signed up

Every portal sign-up is captured with their name, email, role, and organization. You can view and export them yourself.

  1. Go to livehealthychi.com/submissions and sign in with the admin password.
  2. Set the first dropdown to portal-signup to see just the people who registered for the portal (as opposed to contact-form or survey entries).
  3. Use the All roles dropdown to filter by job type — CHW, Nurse, Medical Doctor (MD / DO), NP / PA, Healthcare Admin. This is how you pull, say, just the CHWs when you want to reach one group.
  4. Use Search to find a person or organization, and Export CSV to download the current list (the export respects whatever filters you have set) for a spreadsheet or mail-merge.
Down the road this can grow into letting people subscribe to updates for their specific role. The role field you're filtering on today is the foundation for that — no rework needed when you're ready.

3  Get an event posted

Events are posted for you — no form to wrestle with.

  1. Email the details to LiveHealthyChi@uchicagomedicine.org (or straight to Gary): event name, date, time, location/neighborhood, a sentence or two of description, and a flyer or photo if you have one.
  2. It gets added to the Events section of the site, usually same or next business day. You'll get a note back once it's live.
Batching several events into one email is faster for everyone — and cheaper against the maintenance hours.

4  Add a news story or update

News stories are built as proper pages (good for search and for sharing), so these come through the team too.

  1. Send the headline, the write-up, any photos or video, and links to Gary.
  2. It's built as a shareable story page and added to the News feed. This flows through the maintenance package.
