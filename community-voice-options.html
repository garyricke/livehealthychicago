1 · The Ask

Rachel's request

Excerpted from Rachel Reichlin's email of May 14, 2026 — subject line "LHC Website: Emphasizing Community Co-Governance."

RR
Rachel Reichlin

LHC Website: Emphasizing Community Co-Governance

I'm reaching out to see if you can help the website more strongly depict how community voice is centered in LHC's governance.

Is there something we can highlight in the "how we work" section? Or add a section about our "approach"? Or even Testimonials?

Is it possible to feature the role of the Community Advisory Board in some way (outside of a story on it)? CAB members serve as ambassadors, advisors, and advocates. Their key responsibilities include:

  • Offering feedback on LHC initiatives, strategies, and engagement efforts
  • Sharing community strengths, concerns, and priorities to guide LHC's work
  • Facilitating communication between LHC and neighborhood stakeholders
  • Participating in planning, evaluation, and outreach activities
  • Upholding principles of equity, transparency, and inclusion
  • Supporting the work of the LHC CAB Subcommittees and providing input for Executive and Steering Committee meetings

We have a picture of the CAB and will be having an in-person meeting next week, 5/21 in the morning. During this meeting, we can get more pics and/or testimonials from CAB members about why they participate and the value they offer or receive. Maybe including a little carousel of testimonials from CAB members would be more meaningful and demonstrative than a list of their responsibilities.

— Welcome your ideas and input. Cc'ing Lisa for awareness; Lisa convenes and facilitates the CAB group.

What changes

A fourth pillar — "Community Co-Governance" — joins the existing 3-pillar tab strip. Same auto-advance pattern; same card layout. Click into it to see the CAB photo, responsibilities, and a quote.

Pros

  • Makes governance structurally equal to clinical and public health work — not a footnote.
  • No new section to design; reuses an already-polished component.
  • Visible on every visit because the pillars are central to the homepage narrative.

Cons

  • Breaks the existing "3 pillars" mental model the team has been using in other materials.
  • 4 tabs is tighter on mobile (already had to add a dropdown for the Coalition tabs).
  • Doesn't naturally fit a testimonials carousel — would need a secondary surface.

What changes

A new full-width section is added with four content blocks: framing copy, CAB group photo, 6-icon responsibilities grid, and an auto-advancing testimonials carousel (same pattern as our Coalition tabs).

Pros

  • Highest narrative impact — community voice gets its own dedicated moment instead of being a sub-element.
  • Testimonials carousel is the format Rachel specifically suggested.
  • Rhetorically bridges what we do (Our Model) with where we work (Neighborhoods).
  • Can ship in two phases — section shell with photo+responsibilities before 5/21, testimonials after.

Cons

  • Lengthens the homepage; need to be careful section doesn't feel padded.
  • Needs real content from the 5/21 CAB meeting to feel authentic (otherwise it's responsibilities-as-decoration).
  • Consent forms required for any CAB members whose names/photos appear.

What changes

The Coalition section gains an 8th tab. The tab content displays CAB member headshots in the same grid we use for partner logos, with a short intro paragraph above.

Pros

  • Cheapest to build — the tab strip and mobile dropdown are already in place.
  • Headshots-as-tiles already works visually in the Leadership tab.
  • Tab UI handles "more content added later" gracefully (post-5/21 quotes can drop in).

Cons

  • Conflates partner orgs (institutions) with community members (individuals) — a category error.
  • Buries CAB inside a section that reads as "our institutional partners."
  • Doesn't strengthen the governance message Rachel asked for — it just adds members to a list.
  • Tab strip is already at 7 categories; an 8th makes the desktop overflow we just fixed worse.
Recommendation

Build Option 2 in two phases — ship the shell now, add testimonials after 5/21.

Phase 1 (this week) — section shell, framing copy, CAB group photo, 6-icon responsibilities grid, testimonials carousel placeholder reading "Voices from our CAB — coming soon." Rachel sees something concrete in staging before the in-person meeting.

Phase 2 (after 5/21) — drop in 5–8 real testimonials, member headshots, and any newer group photos captured during Lisa's session. Existing carousel component handles the swap cleanly.

This sequencing means Rachel walks into the 5/21 CAB meeting with a tangible canvas to point at when she's asking members for quotes and photos — instead of describing it abstractly.

5 · Practical Notes

Things to nail down before we build

What to capture at the 5/21 meeting

  • A clean landscape group photo — natural light if possible, no fluorescent overhead glare
  • 5–8 short quotes, ~15–25 words each, on themes like "why I participate," "what I bring," or "what I get back"
  • Individual headshots or candid portraits for each quoted member (1:1 crop, even lighting)
  • Signed consent forms for any member whose name, photo, or quote appears publicly — same issue we have with the mobile-team headshots in core-messaging.html

How this connects to the current site

  • The existing "Community Insight & Mobilization" dashed-border box at the bottom of Our Model becomes a short teaser that anchor-links down to the new section
  • The new section lives between Our Model and 27 Priority Neighborhoods — rhetorically bridges what we do with where we work
  • Auto-advancing testimonials reuse the same React pattern as the Coalition tabs (already in the codebase)
  • Mobile: stacks vertically, photo full-width on top, then responsibilities, then carousel — matches existing section patterns

Budget & scope impact

Estimated ~6–8 hours of build time across phases (shell + content integration). Falls inside the remaining ~$6,250 / ~67 hours of pre-maintenance budget. No scope-change conversation needed — this is in line with the kind of content evolution we've been doing since launch.

See scope.html for budget context.

Open questions for Rachel

  • Any sensitivity about naming individual CAB members publicly, or is the consent question the only gate?
  • Is there a one-line framing statement Lisa or the CAB itself would like to author for the section intro?
